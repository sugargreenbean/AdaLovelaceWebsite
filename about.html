Why do we need this kind of event?

We talk about the “leaky pipeline” a lot. It is the observed phenomenon that women leave tech 4x more often than their male counterparts. We go back and forth about why women are leaving and we have multiple Women In Tech events where we talk even more about these problems. The emphasis on talking instead of tech has negative impacts on an underrepresented minority's career in tech, as evidenced by research at the University of Colorado - Boulder .

Women who have been working in technology for a while want to go to WIT events to learn about technology, and especially about cool tech things done by women. Unfortunately, the vast majority of WIT events are targeted towards entry-level professionals. Recruitment is easier than retention, and it is evident that a demand is not being met for experienced female engineers. There is a desire for more from these WIT events. There needs to be more.

The Ada Lovelace Conference is a conference that targets keeping mid-to-senior level women in tech by giving them a chance to hone and share their skills. This conference doesn't cater to soft-skills or career panels, but instead is full of hands-on workshops where tech is the priority...and it just happens to be made by BAMF-ing women.
